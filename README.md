# Islam Knowledge Base

A comprehensive reference on Islam covering beliefs, practices, history, law, theology, spirituality, and culture. This knowledge base contains detailed, scholarly information suitable for students, scholars, practitioners, and those interested in understanding Islamic civilization.

## Quick Start

**New to this knowledge base?** Start with the [**Comprehensive Guide**](resources/00-index-guide.md) for learning paths and navigation tips.

**Looking for specific information?** See the Resources table below.

## What's Included

This knowledge base covers:

- **Foundational Beliefs**: The Six Articles of Faith, Tawhid (Divine Oneness), and core Islamic theology
- **Practices**: The Five Pillars and Islamic ritual observances
- **Scripture**: The Quran, its revelation, preservation, and interpretation
- **Biography**: The life and example of Prophet Muhammad
- **Law and Jurisprudence**: Islamic legal systems and schools of law
- **History**: 1,400+ years of Islamic civilization and empires
- **Theology**: Islamic beliefs about God, creation, justice, and the hereafter
- **Spirituality**: Sufism and the mystical dimensions of Islam
- **Ethics**: Islamic moral philosophy and character development
- **Finance**: Islamic banking and economics
- **Culture**: Art, architecture, literature, and Islamic aesthetics
- **Modern Context**: Islam in the contemporary world and with other faiths
- **Scientific Perspectives**: Islam's approach to science and knowledge

## Central Concepts

### Tawhid (Divine Oneness)
The cornerstone of Islamic belief and practice. Referenced across all topics as the unifying principle of Islamic thought and action.

### Sunnah (Prophetic Tradition)
The example and teachings of Prophet Muhammad, preserved in hadith collections and practical Islamic tradition. Forms the second source of Islamic law and guidance.

### Sharia (Islamic Law)
The comprehensive system of Islamic guidance covering worship, transactions, criminal law, governance, and all aspects of life. Developed through Quranic principles, Sunnah, and scholarly reasoning.

### Ummah (Islamic Community)
The global Muslim community connected by shared faith, despite differences in culture, language, and geography. Emphasizes unity and collective responsibility.

## Key Quranic Themes

- **Divine Mercy and Justice**: Balance of Allah's mercy and justice
- **Knowledge and Reflection**: Importance of learning and contemplation
- **Community and Brotherhood**: Unity of believers
- **Moral Excellence**: Character and ethical behavior
- **Life Purpose**: Understanding existence and purpose
