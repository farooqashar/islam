# Islamic Art, Architecture, and Culture

## Principles of Islamic Art

- **Aniconism**: avoidance of depicting God, prophets, and living beings in religious contexts
- **Tawhid reflected in art**: unity, order, and harmony as expressions of divine oneness
- **Beauty as worship**: "God is Beautiful and loves beauty" (Sahih Muslim 91)

## Major Art Forms

### Calligraphy (Khatt)

The highest Islamic art form. Major scripts: Kufic, Naskh, Thuluth, Nastaliq, Diwani, Maghribi.

### Geometric Patterns

Complex mathematical patterns including tessellation, star patterns, and girih tiles (quasi-crystalline patterns discovered centuries before Western mathematics described them).

### Arabesque

Flowing vegetal and floral motifs symbolizing the infinite nature of creation.

### Other Arts

- **Miniature Painting**: Persian, Mughal, and Ottoman traditions
- **Ceramics**: Iznik tiles, Moroccan zellij, Persian tile mosaic
- **Textiles**: Persian carpets, Ottoman silk
- **Woodwork**: carved minbars, mashrabiyya screens

## Islamic Architecture

### The Mosque (Masjid)

Key elements: Mihrab (prayer niche), Minbar (pulpit), Minaret, Dome, Sahn (courtyard), Iwan.

### Great Mosques

- **Masjid al-Haram** (Mecca): holiest mosque
- **Masjid al-Nabawi** (Medina): the Prophet's mosque
- **Al-Aqsa Mosque** (Jerusalem): third holiest site
- **Great Mosque of Damascus**, **Great Mosque of Cordoba**, **Al-Azhar Mosque** (Cairo)
- **Sultan Ahmed / Blue Mosque** and **Suleymaniye Mosque** (Istanbul)
- **Shah Mosque** (Isfahan), **Badshahi Mosque** (Lahore)

### Regional Styles

Arabian, Umayyad/Syrian, Abbasid/Iraqi, Andalusian/Moorish, Fatimid/Mamluk, Persian/Iranian, Ottoman/Turkish, Mughal/South Asian, Southeast Asian, West African.

## Literary Culture

- **Arabic**: pre-Islamic poetry, Quranic literary influence, Naguib Mahfouz (Nobel 1988)
- **Persian**: Ferdowsi's Shahnameh, Rumi, Hafiz, Omar Khayyam, Sa'di
- **Turkish**: Divan poetry, Yunus Emre, Orhan Pamuk (Nobel 2006)
- **Urdu**: Mirza Ghalib, Allama Iqbal

## Music

- **Quran recitation**: melodic but not classified as "music"; international competitions
- **Nasheed**: vocal-only religious songs
- **Qawwali**: Sufi devotional music, popularized by Nusrat Fateh Ali Khan
- **Mevlevi ceremony**: whirling dervish ritual
- **Andalusian music** and **Gnawa**: North African traditions
