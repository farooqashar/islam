# Islam in the Modern World

## Demographics

- Approximately **1.9-2 billion** Muslims worldwide (2024 estimates), about 24-25% of the global population
- Largest Muslim-majority country by population: **Indonesia** (~230 million Muslims)
- Approximately **85-90%** of Muslims are Sunni, **10-15%** are Shi'a
- Significant diaspora communities in Europe (~25-30 million), North America (~3.5 million in the US)

## Major Modern Movements

- **Salafi/Wahhabi Movement**: return to Quran and Sunnah, rejection of bid'ah, opposition to Sufism
- **Muslim Brotherhood** (est. 1928): Islamic governance through social activism and political participation
- **Jamaat-e-Islami** (est. 1941): parallel movement in South Asia
- **Deobandi Movement** (est. 1866): traditional Hanafi scholarship emphasizing hadith sciences
- **Barelvi Movement**: love of the Prophet, Sufi practices, shrine visitation
- **Tablighi Jamaat**: apolitical grassroots da'wah movement, possibly the largest Muslim movement globally
- **Islamic Modernism**: reconciling Islamic tradition with modern science, democracy, and human rights

## Islam and Governance

- **Islamic republics**: Iran, Pakistan
- **Monarchies with Islamic law**: Saudi Arabia, Brunei
- **Mixed systems**: Egypt, Malaysia, Jordan, Morocco, Indonesia
- **Secular republics**: Turkey, Tunisia, Central Asian states

## Key Contemporary Issues

- **Islamophobia**: rising anti-Muslim sentiment globally, particularly after 9/11
- **Extremism**: overwhelming majority of scholars condemn terrorism; key refutations include Amman Message (2004), Marrakech Declaration (2016)
- **Women in Islam**: ongoing debates about rights in interpretation vs. cultural practice; multiple female heads of state in Muslim-majority countries
- **Interfaith Relations**: A Common Word (2007), Document on Human Fraternity (2019)
- **Islam and Science**: rich historical legacy; contemporary debates on evolution, bioethics, environmental stewardship

## Major Islamic Institutions

- **Al-Azhar University** (Cairo, est. 970 CE): most prestigious Sunni institution
- **Al-Qarawiyyin** (Fez, Morocco, est. 859 CE): oldest existing university
- **Organization of Islamic Cooperation (OIC)**: 57 member states
- **International Islamic Fiqh Academy** (Jeddah): collective rulings on contemporary issues
