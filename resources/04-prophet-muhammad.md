# The Life of Prophet Muhammad (Seerah)

Muhammad ibn Abdullah (c. 570-632 CE) is the final prophet and messenger of God in Islam.

> "We have not sent you except as a mercy to the worlds." -- Quran 21:107

## Pre-Islamic Arabia (Jahiliyyah)

Tribal society with polytheism, Christianity, Judaism, and Hanifiyyah (Abrahamic monotheism). Mecca was a commercial hub; the Ka'bah housed 360 idols. The Quraysh tribe were custodians of the Ka'bah.

## Early Life (c. 570-610 CE)

Born in the Year of the Elephant. Father Abdullah died before his birth; mother Aminah died when he was 6. Raised by grandfather Abd al-Muttalib then uncle Abu Talib. Known as **al-Amin** (Trustworthy) and **as-Sadiq** (Truthful). Married **Khadijah** at age 25; she was approximately 40. Six children: al-Qasim, Abdullah (both died young), Zaynab, Ruqayyah, Umm Kulthum, Fatimah.

## Prophethood in Mecca (610-622 CE)

- **First revelation** at Cave Hira, age 40: "Read in the name of your Lord..." (96:1-5)
- **Early converts**: Khadijah, Abu Bakr, Ali, Zayd ibn Harithah, Bilal
- **Persecution**: torture of early Muslims (Bilal, Ammar's family -- Sumayyah was the first martyr)
- **Migration to Abyssinia** (615 CE): Muslims sheltered by the Christian Najashi
- **Boycott of Banu Hashim** (616-619 CE)
- **Year of Sorrow** (619 CE): deaths of Abu Talib and Khadijah
- **Isra and Mi'raj**: Night Journey to Jerusalem and ascension through the heavens; five daily prayers prescribed
- **Pledges of Aqabah**: Medinan delegations invite the Prophet to migrate

## The Hijrah and Medina (622-632 CE)

- **Migration to Medina** (622 CE): escaped Quraysh assassination plot; marks Year 1 of the Islamic calendar
- **Masjid an-Nabawi** built; **Constitution of Medina** established multi-faith governance
- **Brotherhood** (Mu'akhah) between Meccan migrants and Medinan helpers

## Major Battles

- **Badr** (624 CE): 313 Muslims defeated ~1,000 Quraysh; decisive early victory
- **Uhud** (625 CE): initial success turned to near-defeat when archers abandoned their posts; Hamzah martyred
- **Battle of the Trench** (627 CE): coalition of 10,000 repelled by a defensive trench
- **Treaty of Hudaybiyyah** (628 CE): ten-year peace; called "a clear victory" (48:1)
- **Conquest of Mecca** (630 CE): ~10,000 fighters, virtually bloodless; general amnesty declared; 360 idols removed from Ka'bah
- **Hunayn** (630 CE) and **Tabuk** (630 CE): final major campaigns

## Key Events

- **Change of Qiblah** (624 CE): from Jerusalem to Ka'bah (2:144)
- **Letters to world leaders**: Heraclius, Khosrow II, Najashi, Muqawqis
- **Farewell Pilgrimage** (632 CE): ~100,000-140,000 pilgrims; the Farewell Sermon proclaimed sanctity of life, racial equality, women's rights
- **Death** (June 8, 632 CE): in Aisha's room; buried in Masjid an-Nabawi

## Personal Life

Married several women after Khadijah's death, mostly widows or divorcees. Key wives include Aisha (scholar, ~2,210 hadith), Hafsah (custodian of the Quran manuscript), Umm Salamah (wise counselor). Known for gentleness, humility, extensive charity, and never striking a woman or servant.

## Legacy

The Sunnah (his practice) is the second source of Islamic law. His teachings transformed Arabian society into a civilization spanning from Spain to Southeast Asia. Major Seerah works: Ibn Ishaq/Ibn Hisham (classical), al-Tabari, Ibn al-Qayyim (Zad al-Ma'ad), Martin Lings (modern English), al-Mubarakpuri (The Sealed Nectar).
