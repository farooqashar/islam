# Islamic Theology (Aqeedah)

## What is Aqeedah

**Aqeedah** (creed/belief) refers to the core doctrines a Muslim holds as matters of faith. While **Fiqh** deals with outward actions, Aqeedah deals with inward belief. **Ilm al-Kalam** (science of discourse) is the discipline of defending and articulating Islamic theology using rational arguments.

## Foundations of Islamic Creed

The **Shahada** ("There is no god but Allah, and Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah") is the theological bedrock.

### Tawhid (Monotheism)

Classical scholars divide Tawhid into three categories:

1. **Tawhid al-Rububiyyah**: Oneness of Lordship - Allah alone creates, sustains, and governs
2. **Tawhid al-Uluhiyyah**: Oneness of Worship - all worship is directed to Allah alone
3. **Tawhid al-Asma wa al-Sifat**: Oneness of Names and Attributes - Allah's names and attributes are unique and perfect

### Shirk (Associating Partners with God)

The gravest sin in Islam. Types include:

- **Major Shirk** (Shirk Akbar): directing worship to other than Allah; takes one outside Islam
- **Minor Shirk** (Shirk Asghar): actions that may lead to major shirk, such as swearing by other than Allah
- **Hidden Shirk**: subtle ostentation in worship (riya)

## Major Sunni Theological Schools

### Athari / Traditionalist School

- Key figures: **Ahmad ibn Hanbal** (d. 855), **Ibn Taymiyyah** (1263-1328), **Ibn al-Qayyim** (d. 1350), **Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab** (d. 1792)
- Methodology: strict adherence to the apparent meanings of the Quran and Sunnah
- On divine attributes: affirm them as they come, "bila kayf" (without asking how), neither literal anthropomorphism nor metaphorical reinterpretation
- Rejects Kalam (dialectical theology) as an innovation
- Modern influence: Salafi movement worldwide

### Ash'ari School

- **Founder**: Abu al-Hasan al-Ash'ari (874-936 CE), a former Mu'tazili who turned against rationalist theology
- Key scholars: **al-Baqillani**, **al-Juwayni**, **al-Ghazali** (d. 1111), **Fakhr al-Din al-Razi** (d. 1209)
- Positions: affirms divine attributes but distinguishes God's attributes from human ones; **occasionalism** (God recreates the universe at every instant); **kasb** (acquisition) theory of human action
- The majority theological school in the Sunni world historically

### Maturidi School

- **Founder**: Abu Mansur al-Maturidi (853-944 CE) from Samarqand
- Similar to Ash'arism on most points, with key differences:
  - Gives greater role to human reason in knowing God
  - Slightly different view on human free will (more agency than Ash'ari kasb)
  - Faith (iman) does not increase or decrease (vs. Ash'ari view that it does)
- Dominant among **Hanafi** scholars (Turkey, Central Asia, South Asia)

### Mu'tazila (Historical)

- Founded by **Wasil ibn Ata** (d. 748) in Basra
- Five principles: Tawhid, Adl (justice), the Promise and the Threat, the Intermediate Position, Commanding Good and Forbidding Evil
- Championed the doctrine that the Quran is **created** (not eternal)
- The **Mihna** (Inquisition, 833-848): Caliph al-Ma'mun imposed the created Quran doctrine; Ahmad ibn Hanbal famously resisted
- Declined after Caliph al-Mutawakkil reversed the Mihna

## Key Theological Debates

### Free Will vs. Predestination

- **Jabriyyah**: complete compulsion; humans have no real choice
- **Qadariyyah / Mu'tazila**: humans have full free will and create their own actions
- **Ash'ari**: God creates all actions; humans "acquire" (kasb) responsibility
- **Maturidi**: humans have a real (though God-given) ability to choose

### The Nature of Faith (Iman)

- **Kharijites**: faith requires action; a major sin removes one from Islam
- **Murji'ah**: faith is belief alone; sin does not affect it
- **Sunni mainstream**: faith comprises belief in the heart, declaration by the tongue, and action by the limbs; a grave sinner remains Muslim but is in spiritual danger

## Shi'a Theology

### Twelver Shi'ism (Ithna Ashariyyah)

- Belief in **Twelve Imams** beginning with Ali ibn Abi Talib and ending with Muhammad al-Mahdi
- **Imamate**: divinely appointed leadership is a pillar of faith; Imams are infallible (ma'sum)
- **Occultation** (Ghaybah): the Twelfth Imam went into hiding in 874 CE and will return as the Mahdi
- **Wilayat al-Faqih**: Ayatollah Khomeini's doctrine that a senior jurist governs in the Imam's absence

### Ismaili Shi'ism

- **Nizari** branch led by the Aga Khan; **Musta'li/Dawoodi Bohra** branch
- Emphasis on esoteric (batini) interpretation of the Quran

### Zaydi Shi'ism

- Follows Zayd ibn Ali (d. 740) as the fifth Imam
- Closest to Sunni theology among Shi'a groups
- Predominantly in Yemen

## Key Creedal Texts

- **al-Fiqh al-Akbar**: attributed to Abu Hanifa, a concise early creed
- **al-Aqidah al-Tahawiyyah** by al-Tahawi (d. 933): the most widely accepted concise Sunni creed
- **al-Aqidah al-Wasitiyyah** by Ibn Taymiyyah: an Athari creedal statement
- **Jawharat al-Tawhid** by al-Laqqani: a versified Ash'ari creed
- **Umm al-Barahin** by al-Sanusi: a popular Ash'ari theological primer
