# Islamic Ethics and Character (Akhlaq)

## Foundations

Ethics in Islam are inseparable from faith and worship. The Prophet Muhammad said: "I was sent to perfect good character" (Innama bu'ithtu li-utammima makarim al-akhlaq - Musnad Ahmad). The Quran describes him: "And indeed, you are of a great moral character" (68:4).

**Taqwa** (God-consciousness) is the foundation of Islamic ethics - the awareness that God sees all actions, which motivates moral behavior beyond mere social convention.

## Key Virtues (Fada'il)

- **Sidq** (truthfulness): "Truthfulness leads to righteousness, and righteousness leads to Paradise" (Sahih al-Bukhari 6094)
- **Amanah** (trustworthiness): the Prophet was known as "al-Amin" even before prophethood
- **Sabr** (patience): "Indeed, Allah is with the patient" (2:153)
- **Shukr** (gratitude): "If you are grateful, I will surely increase you" (14:7)
- **Tawadu** (humility), **Ihsan** (excellence), **Adl** (justice), **Rahmah** (mercy)
- **Hilm** (forbearance), **Haya** (modesty), **Karam** (generosity), **Ikhlas** (sincerity)

## Key Vices (Radha'il)

- **Kibr** (arrogance): "No one who has an atom's weight of arrogance in his heart will enter Paradise" (Sahih Muslim 91)
- **Hasad** (envy), **Riya** (showing off), **Ghibah** (backbiting - compared to eating dead brother's flesh, 49:12)
- **Kidhb** (lying), **Bukhl** (miserliness), **Ghadab** (uncontrolled anger), **Zulm** (oppression)

## Social Ethics

- **Rights of Parents**: "Your Lord has decreed that you worship none but Him, and that you be good to parents" (17:23-24)
- **Rights of Neighbors**: "Jibril kept recommending neighbors to me until I thought he would make them heirs" (Sahih al-Bukhari 6014)
- **Treatment of Animals**: A woman entered Hell for starving a cat; a man was forgiven for giving water to a thirsty dog
- **Environmental Ethics**: Humans are khalifah (stewards) on earth (2:30). "If the Hour is about to be established and one of you has a seedling in his hand, let him plant it" (Musnad Ahmad)
- **Business Ethics**: "The truthful, trustworthy merchant is with the prophets and the martyrs" (al-Tirmidhi 1209)

## Purification of the Soul (Tazkiyat al-Nafs)

"He has succeeded who purifies it, and he has failed who corrupts it" (91:9-10).

Methods: Dhikr, Tafakkur (reflection), Muhasabah (self-accounting), Mujahada (spiritual struggle), Suhba (righteous companionship).

Key works: al-Ghazali's **Ihya Ulum al-Din**, Ibn al-Qayyim's **Madarij al-Salikin**, Ibn Miskawayh's **Tahdhib al-Akhlaq**.

## Ethics of War

- War is a last resort; non-combatants must not be harmed
- Trees and crops must not be destroyed; prisoners treated humanely
- The **greater jihad** is the internal struggle against one's own ego

## Enjoining Good and Forbidding Evil

"Whoever among you sees an evil, let him change it with his hand; if he cannot, then with his tongue; if he cannot, then with his heart - and that is the weakest of faith" (Sahih Muslim 49).
