# Islamic Calendar, Holidays, and Observances

## The Islamic (Hijri) Calendar

A **purely lunar** calendar of 12 months, established by Caliph Umar ibn al-Khattab. Year 1 AH corresponds to the Hijrah (622 CE). A lunar year has approximately **354 or 355 days**, rotating through solar seasons over a ~33-year cycle.

## Major Holidays

### Eid al-Fitr (1 Shawwal)

End of Ramadan. Congregational prayer, Zakat al-Fitr, celebration with family and friends.

### Eid al-Adha (10 Dhul Hijjah)

Festival of Sacrifice coinciding with Hajj. Animal sacrifice (Qurbani), congregational prayer. Considered the greater of the two Eids.

### Ramadan

Month of fasting from dawn to sunset. Taraweeh prayers, increased Quran recitation, Laylat al-Qadr ("better than a thousand months," 97:3), I'tikaf in the last ten days.

### Ashura (10 Muharram)

- **Sunni**: optional fasting commemorating Moses and the Exodus
- **Shi'a**: solemn mourning for the martyrdom of Husayn ibn Ali at Karbala (680 CE)

### Mawlid al-Nabi (12 Rabi al-Awwal)

Prophet's birthday. Celebrated widely but debated (Salafi scholars consider it bid'ah).

## Weekly Observance: Friday (Yawm al-Jumu'ah)

Obligatory congregational prayer (Salat al-Jumu'ah) replacing Dhuhr. "The best day on which the sun rises is Friday" (Sahih Muslim 854).

## Other Observances

- **Day of Arafah** (9 Dhul Hijjah): fasting expiates sins of two years
- **Six days of Shawwal**: fasting these equals fasting the entire year
- **Mondays and Thursdays**: the Prophet regularly fasted on these days
