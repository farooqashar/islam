# Key Islamic Scholars and Figures

## The Companions (Sahabah)

- **Khadijah bint Khuwaylid** (d. 619 CE): first person to accept Islam
- **Aisha bint Abi Bakr** (d. 678 CE): scholar, narrator of ~2,210 hadith
- **Abu Hurayrah** (d. 681): narrated the most hadith (~5,374)
- **Abdullah ibn Abbas** (d. 687): "the scholar of the Ummah," foremost Quran interpreter
- **Khalid ibn al-Walid** (d. 642): "the Sword of God," undefeated military commander
- **Bilal ibn Rabah** (d. ~640): first muezzin, Abyssinian former slave

## Founders of the Legal Schools

- **Abu Hanifa** (699-767 CE): founded the largest school of Fiqh
- **Malik ibn Anas** (711-795 CE): author of al-Muwatta
- **Al-Shafi'i** (767-820 CE): systematized Usul al-Fiqh
- **Ahmad ibn Hanbal** (780-855 CE): champion of hadith-based jurisprudence

## Hadith Masters

- **Al-Bukhari** (810-870 CE): compiled the most authenticated hadith collection
- **Muslim ibn al-Hajjaj** (815-875 CE): author of Sahih Muslim
- **Ibn Hajar al-Asqalani** (1372-1449): Fath al-Bari commentary
- **Al-Nawawi** (1233-1277): Riyad al-Salihin, Forty Hadith

## Theologians and Philosophers

- **Al-Ghazali** (1058-1111): "Hujjat al-Islam," Ihya Ulum al-Din
- **Ibn Rushd / Averroes** (1126-1198): Aristotelian philosopher, shaped European scholasticism
- **Ibn Taymiyyah** (1263-1328): influential on modern Salafi thought
- **Ibn al-Qayyim** (1292-1350): prolific author on theology, fiqh, and spirituality

## Scientists and Polymaths

- **Al-Khwarizmi** (c. 780-850): "father of algebra"
- **Ibn Sina / Avicenna** (980-1037): Canon of Medicine
- **Ibn al-Haytham** (965-1040): "father of optics"
- **Ibn Khaldun** (1332-1406): founder of sociology and historiography

## Sufi Masters

- **Rumi** (1207-1273): author of the Masnavi
- **Ibn Arabi** (1165-1240): "the Greatest Master," Wahdat al-Wujud
- **Abdul Qadir al-Jilani** (1077-1166): founder of the Qadiriyyah order

## Modern Scholars

- **Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab** (1703-1792): Arabian revivalist
- **Jamal al-Din al-Afghani** (1838-1897): Pan-Islamism pioneer
- **Muhammad Abduh** (1849-1905): Egyptian reformer
- **Hasan al-Banna** (1906-1949): founded the Muslim Brotherhood
- **Sayyid Qutb** (1906-1966): influential Islamist thinker
- **Al-Albani** (1914-1999): modern hadith re-grading scholar
- **Hamza Yusuf** (b. 1958): co-founder of Zaytuna College
- **Timothy Winter / Abdal Hakim Murad** (b. 1960): Cambridge scholar
