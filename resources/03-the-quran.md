# The Quran (Al-Qur'an)

The Quran (Arabic: "The Recitation") is the central religious text of Islam. Muslims believe it is the literal word of God revealed to Prophet Muhammad through Angel Jibril over approximately 23 years (610-632 CE).

> "Indeed, it is We who sent down the Quran, and indeed, We will be its guardian." -- Quran 15:9

## Structure

- **114 Surahs** (chapters), arranged roughly by length (longest to shortest)
- **~6,236 Ayat** (verses)
- **30 Juz** (parts) for monthly recitation, **7 Manzil** for weekly recitation
- Longest surah: al-Baqarah (286 verses). Shortest: al-Kawthar (3 verses)

## Revelation

First revelation in 610 CE at Cave Hira: "Read in the name of your Lord who created..." (96:1-5). Meccan surahs (~86): shorter, focus on theology, prophethood, resurrection. Medinan surahs (~28): longer, focus on legislation, social laws.

## Compilation and Preservation

- **During the Prophet's lifetime**: scribes recorded revelations; primary preservation through oral memorization
- **Under Abu Bakr** (632-634): Zayd ibn Thabit compiled the first manuscript after many memorizers died at Yamama
- **Under Uthman** (644-656): standardized the Uthmani Mushaf, sent copies to major cities
- **Qira'at**: multiple valid readings (7/10 canonical), most common today is Hafs 'an Asim

## Key Themes

Monotheism (Tawhid), prophethood, eschatology, law and ethics, stories of previous nations, social justice, family law, warfare and peace. Approximately 500 verses with explicit legal content.

## Major Surahs

- **Al-Fatiha** (1): "Mother of the Book," recited in every prayer
- **Al-Baqarah** (2): longest surah, contains Ayat al-Kursi (2:255)
- **Yasin** (36): called "the Heart of the Quran"
- **Ar-Rahman** (55): "So which of the favors of your Lord would you deny?"
- **Al-Mulk** (67): protects from punishment of the grave
- **Al-Kahf** (18): recommended for Friday recitation
- **Al-Ikhlas** (112): pure monotheism, equals one-third of the Quran in reward

## Sciences of the Quran (Ulum al-Quran)

### Tafsir (Exegesis)

Major works: al-Tabari (foundational), Ibn Kathir (hadith-based), al-Qurtubi (legal focus), al-Zamakhshari (linguistic), Fakhr al-Din al-Razi (philosophical), Sayyid Qutb (modern literary), Ibn Ashur (maqasid-focused).

### I'jaz (Inimitability)

The Quran challenges anyone to produce something comparable (17:88, 2:23-24). Aspects: linguistic, legislative, scientific, historical, psychological.

### Nasikh and Mansukh (Abrogation)

Some legal rulings superseded by later revelations (2:106). Scope debated among scholars.

## Translations

Orthodox view: translations are interpretations, not the Quran itself. Notable English translations: Pickthall (1930), Yusuf Ali (1934), Muhammad Asad (1980), Sahih International (1997), Abdel Haleem (2004), Mustafa Khattab (2016).

## The Quran in Daily Life

Memorization (Hifz) is a revered achievement; an estimated 10+ million Huffaz alive today. Famous reciters include Abdul Basit, Mishary Alafasy, Abdul Rahman al-Sudais. Tajweed governs the rules of proper recitation.
