# The Five Pillars of Islam (Arkan al-Islam)

> "Islam is built upon five: testifying that there is no god but Allah and that Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah, establishing prayer, paying Zakat, making Hajj to the House, and fasting in Ramadan."
> -- Sahih al-Bukhari 8

## 1. Shahada -- The Declaration of Faith

**Arabic**: Ashhadu an la ilaha illa Allah, wa ashhadu anna Muhammadan rasul Allah

Two parts: (1) La ilaha illa Allah -- no god but Allah; (2) Muhammad is the Messenger of Allah. Seven conditions: knowledge, certainty, acceptance, submission, truthfulness, sincerity, love.

## 2. Salah -- The Five Daily Prayers

| Prayer | Time | Rak'at |
|---|---|---|
| Fajr | Dawn to sunrise | 2 |
| Dhuhr | After zenith | 4 |
| Asr | Afternoon | 4 |
| Maghrib | Sunset | 3 |
| Isha | Night | 4 |

**Prerequisites**: Wudu (ablution), covering awrah, facing Qiblah, entering prayer time, intention. **Procedure**: Takbir, Qiyam (standing with Fatiha), Ruku (bowing), Sujud (prostration), Tashahhud, Taslim.

**Other prayers**: Jumu'ah (Friday), Eid prayers, Taraweeh (Ramadan), Tahajjud (night), Janazah (funeral), Istikhara (guidance).

## 3. Zakat -- Obligatory Charity

2.5% annual tax on wealth exceeding the Nisab (85g gold or 595g silver equivalent). Also applies to livestock, agricultural produce, and trade goods.

**Eight recipients** (Quran 9:60): the poor, the needy, zakat collectors, those whose hearts are to be reconciled, freeing captives, those in debt, in the cause of Allah, the wayfarer.

**Zakat al-Fitr**: paid at end of Ramadan before Eid prayer, ~2.5-3 kg of staple food per person.

## 4. Sawm -- Fasting During Ramadan

Abstaining from food, drink, smoking, and sexual intercourse from dawn (Fajr) to sunset (Maghrib) during the ninth lunar month.

**Exempt**: children, the elderly, chronically ill, temporarily ill (make up later), travelers, pregnant/nursing women, menstruating women.

**Laylat al-Qadr** (Night of Decree): "better than a thousand months" (97:3), sought in the odd nights of the last ten days.

**I'tikaf**: spiritual retreat in the mosque during the last ten days.

## 5. Hajj -- Pilgrimage to Mecca

Obligatory once in a lifetime for those physically and financially able. Performed 8th-13th of Dhul Hijjah.

### Rituals

1. **Ihram**: sacred state, two white cloths for men, Talbiyah recitation
2. **Mina**: day of preparation (8th)
3. **Arafah**: the essential pillar -- standing in supplication from noon to sunset (9th). "Hajj is Arafah"
4. **Muzdalifah**: night under open sky, collect pebbles
5. **Stoning of Jamarat**: rejecting Satan's temptation (10th)
6. **Animal sacrifice** (Hady) and **shaving/cutting hair**
7. **Tawaf al-Ifadah**: circumambulating Ka'bah seven times
8. **Sa'i**: walking between Safa and Marwah seven times
9. **Days of Tashreeq** (11th-13th): stoning all three Jamarat
10. **Tawaf al-Wada**: farewell circumambulation

**Umrah** (lesser pilgrimage) can be performed anytime: Ihram, Tawaf, Sa'i, hair cutting.

Approximately 2-3 million pilgrims perform Hajj annually under a quota system.
