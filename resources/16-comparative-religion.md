# Islam and Other Faiths

## The Abrahamic Connection

Islam considers itself the final revelation in the Abrahamic tradition, sharing roots with Judaism and Christianity. "Say, 'We believe in Allah and what was revealed to us, and what was revealed to Abraham, Ishmael, Isaac, Jacob...'" (3:84).

## People of the Book (Ahl al-Kitab)

Jews and Christians have special status: historically given protected (Dhimmi) status, free to practice their religion, required to pay Jizyah in lieu of military service and Zakat. Muslim men may marry Jewish or Christian women (5:5). The Constitution of Medina (622 CE) established a multi-faith political community.

## Islam and Judaism

**Shared**: strict monotheism, prophetic tradition, divine law (Shariah/Halakha), dietary laws, circumcision, prohibition of images of God.

**Differences**: Islam considers Muhammad the final prophet; the Quran states previous scriptures were altered (Tahrif). Historical relations include significant cooperation (Al-Andalus) alongside periods of tension.

## Islam and Christianity

**Shared**: God as Creator, reverence for Jesus and Mary, virgin birth of Jesus, Jesus as Messiah, belief in Second Coming, heaven/hell, angels, Day of Judgment.

**Differences**: Islam rejects the Trinity (4:171), the divinity of Jesus (5:72), the crucifixion (4:157-158), and original sin. Salvation comes through faith and deeds, not vicarious atonement.

## Key Quranic Principles on Religious Diversity

- "There is no compulsion in religion" (2:256)
- "For you is your religion, and for me is my religion" (109:6)
- "We have made you into nations and tribes so that you may know one another" (49:13)
- "So compete in good deeds" (5:48)

## Modern Interfaith Dialogue

- **A Common Word Between Us and You** (2007): 138 Muslim scholars to Christian leaders
- **Amman Message** (2004): affirming validity of Islamic schools, condemning takfir
- **Marrakech Declaration** (2016): rights of religious minorities in Muslim-majority countries
- **Document on Human Fraternity** (Abu Dhabi, 2019): Pope Francis and Grand Imam Ahmad al-Tayeb
